ClaimFlow Analytics – Healthcare Revenue Cycle Dashboard
================================================================================

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------
Project Name: ClaimFlow Analytics
Domain: Healthcare Revenue Cycle Management
Client Persona: SettleMed (Noida-based healthcare billing startup)
Owner: Pushpal Kawara
Portfolio: Under development

Objective: Provide a centralized analytics dashboard to monitor,
investigate, and recover denied insurance claims, reducing revenue
leakage and improving billing efficiency.

2. BUSINESS CONTEXT & PROBLEM STATEMENT
--------------------------------------------------
- US hospitals lose billions annually due to claim denials and underpayments.
- Billing teams lack real-time visibility into:
  * Which payers deny claims and why
  * Amount of cash stuck in pending/denied status
  * Hospital-wise denial performance
  * Financial impact of denials and recovery potential
- SettleMed required a single source of truth to:
  * Track denial patterns
  * Prioritize high-value recoveries
  * Guide process improvements

3. DATA REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------
- Data Source: Synthetically generated using Python (Pandas, Faker)
- Time Period: Jan 2024 – Dec 2025
- Volume: ~50,000 claims
- Entities:
  * Hospitals: 25+ facilities across US states
  * Payers: 12 insurance providers (commercial & government)
- Fact Table: fact_claims
  Columns: Claim_ID, Hospital_ID, Payer_ID, Date_ID,
  Claim_Amount, Paid_Amount, Denial_Reason, Claim_Status,
  Days_Pending, Appeal_Flag, Recovery_Amount, etc.
- Dimension Tables:
  * dim_hospitals (Name, State, Bed_Size, Type)
  * dim_payers (Payer_Name, Category)
  * dim_date (Date, Month, Quarter, Year, Fiscal attributes)
- Data Generation Logic:
  * Realistic denial rates (15-18% for commercial payers)
  * Common denial reasons (missing documentation, medical necessity, coding errors)
  * Aging distribution replicating real revenue cycles
  * Appeal success rates around 50%
  * Hospital performance variation

4. DATA MODELING (STAR SCHEMA)
--------------------------------------------------
- Fact table: fact_claims at center
- Dimensions: dim_hospitals, dim_payers, dim_date
- 1-to-many relationships
- Custom DAX date table for time intelligence
- Optimized for fast cross-filtering and drill-through

6. KEY DAX MEASURES (20+)
--------------------------------------------------
Total Claims = COUNTROWS(fact_claims)
Denied Claims = CALCULATE([Total Claims], Claim_Status = "Denied")
Denial Rate = DIVIDE([Denied Claims], [Total Claims])
Revenue Gap = SUMX(fact_claims, Claim_Amount - Paid_Amount)
Pending Amount = CALCULATE(SUM(fact_claims[Claim_Amount]), Claim_Status = "Pending")
Recovery % = DIVIDE(SUM(fact_claims[Recovery_Amount]), SUM(fact_claims[Claim_Amount]))
Appeal Success Rate = DIVIDE([Recovered Denied], [Appealed Denied])
MoM Growth = (Current Month - Previous Month)/Previous Month
Data Quality Flag = COUNTROWS(fact_claims) where denial reason is blank
Avg. Pending Days = AVERAGE(fact_claims[Days_Pending])

7. DATA CLEANING (POWER QUERY)
--------------------------------------------------
Six automated steps:
1. Remove duplicate claim IDs
2. Standardize payer names (mixed casing)
3. Fill missing denial reasons with "Not Specified"
4. Correct negative claim amounts
5. Convert date columns to proper date type
6. Create derived columns (Claim_Status_Group, Age_Bucket)
